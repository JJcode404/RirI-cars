# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Kenyan car buyers — primarily in Nairobi and surrounding areas — shopping for reliable, affordable Japanese imports on mid-range Android devices. They are practical decision-makers: they want to know what's in stock, what it costs, and whether this dealership can be trusted. Many arrive from Facebook or WhatsApp. They compare several dealers before committing to a visit or inquiry.

## Product Purpose

Riri Cars Ltd.'s website is the digital front desk for a physical showroom on Kiambu Road. Its job is to surface the right car quickly, establish trust, and convert interest into a WhatsApp message, phone call, or showroom visit. Success is measured in inquiries, not page impressions.

## Brand Personality

Trusted · Approachable · Local

Voice: straightforward and warm — the tone of a knowledgeable friend who works at the dealership, not a sales script. Active, specific, Nairobi-rooted copy. No fluff.

## Anti-references

- **Generic used-car lots**: cluttered layouts, too many rotating banners, mismatched fonts, cheap badge stacks, price-sticker aesthetics. Low trust, high noise.
- **Western big-box auto catalog sites** (CarMax, AutoTrader): impersonal, corporate, loses the local personality that is Riri's differentiator.
- **AI-scaffold templates**: cream backgrounds, repeated uppercase eyebrow labels above every section, identical card grids with icon + heading + text, gradient text, glassmorphism as filler.

## Design Principles

1. **Inventory clarity first.** The fastest path from landing to finding a car wins. Every layout, nav, and CTA decision should reduce friction between a visitor and their next vehicle.
2. **Trust through specificity.** Real numbers, real location, real documentation. Auction paperwork, financing options, and contact details should be visible and credible — not buried.
3. **Locally grounded.** Nairobi context is a strength. KES pricing, local landmark references (Kiambu Road, Ridgeways, Fourways Junction), and familiar social channels (WhatsApp, Facebook) create belonging and signal legitimacy.
4. **Mobile-native, performance-first.** Kenyan users browse on mid-range Android. Touch targets, image weight, and layout density are product requirements, not enhancements.
5. **Warmth over spectacle.** Approachable and honest beats flashy. Avoid clutter, aggressive sales patterns, and visual noise that erodes confidence in the brand.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target: WCAG 2.1 AA
- Body text must achieve ≥4.5:1 contrast against its background (the existing `muted` (#6B7280) on `brand-bg` (#F7F7F7) is close to the limit — verify and bump if needed)
- Keyboard navigation and visible focus indicators required on all interactive elements
- Forms and search must be operable without a mouse
- No autoplay media; reduced-motion preference respected in all transitions
- Alt text on all images, written in brand voice (specific, not generic)
